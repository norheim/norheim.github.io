Johannes Norheim

Space systems engineering · Digital continuity · AI for complex systems

Johannes Norheim is an Assistant Professor and Chancellor's Fellow in Computational Engineering Design in the Department of Design, Manufacturing, and Engineering Management (DMEM) at the University of Strathclyde in Scotland.

Before joining Strathclyde, he earned his bachelor's and master's degrees in Aeronautics and Astronautics and his Ph.D. in Engineering Systems at MIT, then worked as a postdoctoral researcher in MIT's Engineering Systems Lab until August 2024.

His research focuses on accelerating the development of complex systems by enabling digital continuity across the entire systems engineering lifecycle. In particular, Johannes works at the intersection of design, simulation, optimization, and generative AI methods. His past research work has received funding from the European Space Agency, Airbus and NASA, as well as the UK Engineering & Physical Sciences Research Council.
